Postdoctoral Researcher · Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory

Dr.AdrienFrémont

Rhizosphere Metabolomics & Microbial Ecology  ·  Plant–Soil Chemodiversity  ·  Agroecology

I study how root chemistry shapes soil ecosystems. From the metabolites plants secrete into the rhizosphere to the microbial communities they recruit and the ecosystem services they sustain. My research integrates untargeted metabolomics, metagenomics, and multi-omics modelling to decode the molecular signals of plant-soil-microbe interactions and harness them for sustainable agriculture.

Arsenic Detoxification via Root Exudation
Discovery of a previously unknown plant detoxification mechanism: white lupin actively exudes arsenic-phytochelatin complexes into the rhizosphere, redefining our understanding of plant arsenic tolerance.
